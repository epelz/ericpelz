Hello!

I'm a senior at the California Institute of Technology (Caltech), double majoring in Computer Science and Business Economics and Management.

Experience

This past summer, I was a Software Engineer Intern at Asana in San Francisco, CA.

I spent the previous summer at Facebook in Menlo Park, CA, where I developed backend services for a then-unreleased product, Nearby Friends, using Java, HBase, Thrift, and PHP.

Before that, in the summer of 2012, I was a Data Science Intern at Adaptly in New York City, working on problems in ad optimization and sentiment analysis for social media. Even further back, in 2010 and 2011, I created online graphical Monte-Carlo simulations for the Bad Metals Research Group at the National High Magnetic Field Laboratory in Tallahassee, Florida (article).

At Caltech, I am the lead developer of Donut, our undergraduate web portal, and a Ruddock House Representative of Information Management Systems and Services (IMSS) at Caltech. In these positions, I lead teams to maintain and create applications for students. I was also a Teaching Assistant for CS 115 (Functional Programming) in Spring 2014, CS 121 (Introduction to Relational Databases) in Fall 2013 and 2014, and CS 2 (Introduction to Programming Methods) in Winter 2013.
