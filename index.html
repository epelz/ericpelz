I'm a sophomore at the California Institute of Technology (Caltech), double majoring in Computer Science and Business, Economics, and Management.

Experience

I am currently a Data Science Intern at Adaptly, working under the mentorship of Michael Betancourt, Ph.D.

At Caltech, I am the lead developer of Donut, our undergraduate web portal, and a Ruddock House Representative of Information Management Systems and Services (IMSS) at Caltech.

Between June 2010 and July 2011, I was a member of the Bad Metals Research Group under the direction of Professor Vladimir Dobrosavljevic at the National High Magnetic Field Laboratory in Tallahassee, Florida.

I created online models to simulate real-time dynamics of various conditions of Nearly Frozen Coulomb Liquids, the coulomb-limited phase separation, and the nuclear "pasta" phase of a neutron star. These simulations utilized Monte-Carlo simulation code to visualize the electron dynamics near charge ordering transitions and were written in the Java programming language each with approximately 2,000 lines of code.

In Summer 2011, I mentored two gifted high school seniors in creating online simulations of resistive switching in memristors in extensive collaboration with Professors Marcelo Rozenberg and Gergely Zimanyi. Additionally, using Fortran and the FSU High Performance Computing cluster, I simulated coulomb liquids of various conditions to explore and calculate conductivity and critical temperatures.

I was featured in the Tallahassee Democrat, the NHMFL, and FSU Physics in September 2010.
